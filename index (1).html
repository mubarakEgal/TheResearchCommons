About the programme

What is MERIDIAN?

MERIDIAN is a structured summer research programme open to City St George's students. You will contribute to one of two global mental health research projects led by an experienced researcher, with the goal of completing the manuscript before the new academic year. The paper will then be submitted for peer review and published in due course.

All work is remote and designed to fit around your summer. If you contribute meaningfully, you will be named as a co-author on the final paper.

How the summer breaks down

Done before September

Now — late May
Register your interest. You receive a project brief and short application form. Places confirmed on a rolling basis until full.
June — onboarding
One 90-minute online session. Introduction to your project, your team, the methodology, and what co-authorship requires. Recorded if you cannot attend live.
June – July
Active contribution phase. Screening, data extraction, or coding depending on your project. Async-first — work around travel and other commitments.
August
Write-up and review. You contribute to and review the manuscript. Co-authorship confirmed for contributors who meet the threshold.
End of August
Manuscript complete. Paper ready for submission before the new academic year. Your contribution formally documented — usable in applications from September.

Places are capped at 5 per project. Meaningful mentorship does not scale beyond that. Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis — the cohort closes as soon as places are filled. There is no guarantee of a second cohort this year.

Common questions

Things worth knowing

Can I do this while travelling or working a summer job?
Yes. The programme is async-first — around 6 hours per week, broken up however suits you. There are no mandatory live sessions beyond onboarding, which is recorded. The contribution window is wide enough to accommodate plans.
What if the paper is not accepted before I need to use it in applications?
Submission is the milestone. You can reference a submitted manuscript in applications from the moment it is submitted, and your contribution will be formally documented in writing regardless of journal timeline.
What counts as meaningful contribution for co-authorship?
Each project has a written contribution framework set out before you start. The threshold is designed to be achievable within the stated time commitment. There are no surprises.
Do I need prior research experience?
No. The programme is accessible from a zero research background. Training is built in. Motivation and reliability matter more than prior knowledge.
What are the research topics?
Both projects sit within global mental health. Full project briefs are shared with registered applicants before the cohort begins.
